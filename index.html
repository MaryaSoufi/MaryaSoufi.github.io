Full case study — Sarnex

The problem

Users needed to complete critical tasks without wading through dense information or ambiguous navigation. The experience had to feel credible and calm while surfacing the right actions at the right time—especially for people who visit intermittently and cannot relearn the product on every session.

My role

End-to-end UX and UI design support for AREX: structuring screens and flows, refining interface copy and hierarchy, prototyping in Figma, and partnering with developers on GitHub to align implementation with interaction intent and accessibility considerations.

Process

  • Mapped primary user journeys and edge cases to reduce dead-ends and redundant steps.
  • Iterated low- to mid-fidelity layouts before locking visual patterns and component behavior.
  • Documented states (default, empty, error, loading) so engineering could ship predictably.

Research & validation

Combined product requirements with heuristic review and feedback from stakeholders and internal users. Adjusted layouts when we saw confusion around labeling, hierarchy, or where users expected to land after key actions.

Solution & iteration

Delivered a clearer information architecture and a more consistent UI system across core flows. Refined components through implementation feedback—tightening spacing, copy, and affordances once real data and edge cases appeared in the build.

Outcomes

Stronger alignment between design intent and shipped UI, faster review cycles with engineering, and a product surface that internal reviewers described as easier to scan and act on. Specific metrics and additional artifacts are available on request where confidentiality allows.

Full case study — CRM website

The problem

CRM users were losing time switching between views, hunting for record context, and repeating manual steps. The website needed to support heavy daily use without cognitive overload, while still flexible enough for different roles (viewing, updating, reporting).

My role

UX and content design for key pages and flows: navigation model, list and detail patterns, forms and validation messaging, and dashboard-style summaries. Collaborated with developers to ensure responsive behavior and consistent components.

Process

  • Audited existing flows and prioritized the highest-frequency tasks for redesign.
  • Proposed a clearer hierarchy for record data, actions, and history so scanning beats scrolling.
  • Standardized labels, empty states, and microcopy to reduce ambiguity and support training.

Research & validation

Used interviews and informal walkthroughs with people who use the CRM daily. Stress-tested layouts against realistic record complexity and messy data. Iterated table density, filters, and primary actions based on where users hesitated or backtracked.

Solution & iteration

Delivered updated page templates and interaction patterns that shorten common paths and expose context earlier in the journey. Followed up post-launch with tweaks to spacing, column priority, and terminology as usage patterns emerged.

Outcomes

More predictable navigation between records and tasks, clearer affordances for primary actions, and qualitative feedback that the interface feels calmer and easier to teach to new team members.
